# Wooclap Form 2: Presentation & Q&A (Writing 2 + Writing 3)
**Purpose:** Get colleague buy-in on what the P+Q&A should focus on and how it should be structured.
**Estimated time:** 5–7 minutes
**Phase:** 1 (Big-picture assessment decisions)

---

## Context Block (shown at the start of the form)

> Both Writing 2 and Writing 3 use a **Presentation + Q&A** (20 min total: 15 min active + 5 min buffer) to validate authorship and assess writing strategy awareness. Students submit their take-home text one week in advance, then present and defend their work.
>
> **Writing 2 (B2/C1):** Students present their creative mimicry piece — a continuation or missing scene (1000–1500 words) imitating a published author's voice. They also explain 3 highlighted grammatical structures.
>
> **Writing 3 (C1/C2):** Students present their proposal — a formal, argumentative text addressed to a professional audience (e.g., a school board). The timed test already covers improvisational writing skills, so the P+Q&A can focus on different competencies.
>
> The key design constraint: **15 minutes of active time per student**. We can't cover everything — we need to make smart choices about what to focus on.

---

## Questions

### Q1 — Writing 2 P+Q&A Focus (MCQ — pick top 3)
**Type:** MCQ (multiple answers, pick 3)
**Question:** In 15 minutes, which areas should the Writing 2 P+Q&A focus on? Pick your **top 3**:

- Source text understanding — can the student describe the original author's style and techniques?
- Style imitation decisions — how did the student adapt tone, voice, and pacing to match the original?
- Plot and characterisation — does the new chapter fit the book's narrative and characters?
- Writing process and strategy — can the student explain *why* they made the choices they made?
- Grammar explanation — can the student identify and explain the 3 highlighted grammatical structures?
- Authorship validation — does the student convincingly demonstrate they wrote the text?

---

### Q2 — Writing 3 P+Q&A Focus (MCQ — pick top 3)
**Type:** MCQ (multiple answers, pick 3)
**Question:** The Writing 3 timed test already assesses improvisational writing and language level. Given that, which areas should the P+Q&A focus on? Pick your **top 3**:

- Thesis and argument structure — why these arguments, why this structure?
- Source selection and reliability — why these sources over others?
- Body paragraph development — how a specific paragraph is built (topic sentence, evidence, development)
- Genre awareness — how the student adhered to proposal conventions (register, audience, formality)
- Writing strategy decisions — the student's process from idea to finished text
- Authorship validation — does the student convincingly demonstrate they wrote the proposal?

---

### Q3 — Presentation Structure (Poll)
**Type:** Poll
**Question:** Should students follow a fixed checklist of points to cover, or present more freely?

- **Fixed checklist** — students get a list of required points to address; ensures consistency across students and examiners
- **Semi-structured** — students get guiding themes but choose how to present; the examiner uses Q&A to fill gaps
- **Free-form** — students decide what to present; the examiner steers through questions

---

### Q4 — The Examiner's Role (Poll)
**Type:** Poll
**Question:** During the Q&A portion, how active should the examiner be?

- **Mostly listening** — the student drives; the examiner only asks clarification questions
- **Guided conversation** — the examiner has prepared questions and steers the conversation to cover key areas
- **Probing** — the examiner actively tests depth by asking follow-up questions, pushing the student to go beyond surface-level answers

### Q5 — Writing 2 Rubric Review (Open Question)
**Type:** Open Question
**Question:** Here are the proposed rubric criteria for Writing 2's P+Q&A. Anything missing, overweighted, or unclear?

> | Criterion | At Level (B2+) | Above Level (C1+) |
> |-----------|----------------|-------------------|
> | **Genre Awareness & Application** | Explains conventions; demonstrates awareness of audience; applies genre features | Nuanced understanding; evaluates effectiveness of genre choices |
> | **Strategy Articulation** | Articulates methods used; uses terminology; gives examples | Evaluates strategy effectiveness; discusses trade-offs |
> | **Grammatical Awareness & Language Level** | Identifies and explains B2/C1 structures in own work; language at B2+ | Sophisticated grammatical insight; language at C1+ |

---

### Q6 — Writing 3 P+Q&A Rubric Review (Open Question)
**Type:** Open Question
**Question:** Here are the proposed rubric criteria for Writing 3's P+Q&A. Anything missing, overweighted, or unclear?

> | Criterion | What it covers |
> |-----------|---------------|
> | **Authorship & Language Level** | Student demonstrates they authored a clear, complex text. Spoken and written English at C1/C2. |
> | **Strategy Justification** | Student articulates writing strategies, evaluates their effectiveness, and discusses trade-offs. |
> | **Decision-Making** | Student explains rhetorical and structural choices — how they leveraged purpose and audience. |
> | **Genre Application** | Text follows formal proposal conventions; student can articulate what those are and why they matter. |
