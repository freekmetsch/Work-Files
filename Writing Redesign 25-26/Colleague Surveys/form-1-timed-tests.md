# Wooclap Form 1: Timed Tests (Writing 1 + Writing 3)
**Purpose:** Get colleague buy-in on the timed test format, assessment criteria, and priorities.
**Estimated time:** 5–7 minutes
**Phase:** 1 (Big-picture assessment decisions)

---

## Context Block (shown at the start of the form)

> We're redesigning the writing courses for 2026-2027. Writing 1 and Writing 3 both include a **timed test** as a core assessment. The format closely follows Cambridge exam conventions:
>
> **Writing 1 (B1/B2):** 90 minutes, two texts (Cambridge B2 First format). Part 1 is a compulsory essay (140–190 words). Part 2 is a choice from text types like articles, emails/letters, reports, or reviews (140–190 words).
>
> **Writing 3 (C1/C2):** 90 minutes, two texts (Cambridge C1 Advanced format). Part 1 is a compulsory essay with argument development (220–260 words). Part 2 is a choice from text types like proposals, reports, reviews, or letters (220–260 words).
>
> Both tests are digital (BYOD, secured environment via Schoolyear + TestVision). No aids allowed.

### Q2 — Writing 1: Assessment Priorities (Ranking)
**Type:** Ranking
**Question:** For the Writing 1 timed test (B1/B2), rank these assessment criteria from most to least important:

- **Task response** — Has the student written what was asked? Are they writing intentionally for the given purpose and audience?
- **Grammar and diction** — Is the language appropriate for B1/B2 level? Do errors impede communication?
- **Coherent structure** — Is the text logically organised with clear paragraph unity? (Note: expectations are modest given the time pressure and short text length)
- **Genre awareness** — Does the student show awareness of the text type's conventions? (Note: CEFR only makes genre awareness explicitly prominent at C1)

---

### Q3 — Writing 3: Assessment Priorities (Ranking)
**Type:** Ranking
**Question:** For the Writing 3 timed test (C1/C2), rank these assessment criteria from most to least important:

- **Communicative effectiveness** — Does the text achieve its purpose with sophisticated audience awareness and an effective style?
- **Writing strategy application** — Are writing strategies visible in the product (argument structure, reader guidance, rhetorical techniques)?
- **Language level** — Does the writing demonstrate C1/C2 level with consistent grammatical control, varied vocabulary, and natural style?
- **Genre awareness** — Does the student strategically leverage genre conventions for the chosen text type?
