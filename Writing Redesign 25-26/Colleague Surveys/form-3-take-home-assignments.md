# Wooclap Form 3: Take-Home Assignments (Writing 2 + Writing 3)
**Purpose:** Get colleague buy-in on the take-home assignment designs, book list, and the proposal-only decision for W3.
**Estimated time:** 5–7 minutes
**Phase:** 1 (Big-picture assessment decisions)

---

## Context Block (shown at the start of the form)

> The take-home assignments are the "unsecured" lane of our dual-lane assessment strategy. Students produce a polished text at home (AI tools allowed), then defend it in the P+Q&A. The P+Q&A is where authorship and process are validated.
>
> **Writing 2 — Creative Mimicry (B2/C1):**
> Students select a chapter from a published literary work and write a continuation or missing scene (1000–1500 words) mimicking the original author's voice. They also highlight 3 grammatical structures (simple, medium, complex) to explain during the P+Q&A. Students submit with an author declaration form one week before presenting.
>
> **Writing 3 — Proposal (C1/C2):**
> Students write a formal, argumentative proposal addressed to a professional audience (e.g., a school board at their internship school). They propose a course of action, support it with reasoned arguments, and demonstrate C1/C2 writing. The creative option (Murderless Mystery / Personal Essay) has been removed — the take-home is now proposal-only. Students submit with an author declaration form one week before presenting.

### Q5 — Assignment Description Review: Writing 2 (Open Question)
**Type:** Open Question
**Question:** Here's the draft assignment description for Writing 2. Anything unclear, missing, or that you'd change?

> **The assignment**
> You select a chapter from a published literary work and write a **continuation or missing scene** (1000–1500 words) that mimics the original author's voice. Your text should be well-structured and demonstrate that you can adapt your tone, style, and register to match the source text's audience and purpose.
>
> **Grammar element**
> You highlight **three grammatical structures** in your text — one simple, one medium, one complex (from the selection list provided during the course). During the P+Q&A, you identify and explain these structures as applied in your own writing.
>
> **AI policy:** You may use AI tools to polish and refine your text, but you must remain the author. Your ownership of the content and creative decisions is validated during the P+Q&A.

### Q6 — Assignment Description Review: Writing 3 Proposal (Open Question)
**Type:** Open Question
**Question:** Here's the draft assignment description for the Writing 3 proposal. Anything unclear, missing, or that you'd change?

> **The assignment**
> You write a **proposal** — a formal, argumentative text aimed at a professional audience. You identify a problem or opportunity, present a well-reasoned case, and propose a course of action. The proposal should demonstrate clear, complex writing in an effective style, with consistent grammatical control at C1/C2 level.
>
> **AI policy:** You may use AI tools to polish and refine your proposal, but you must remain the author. Your ownership of the content, argumentation, and structural decisions is validated during the P+Q&A.
