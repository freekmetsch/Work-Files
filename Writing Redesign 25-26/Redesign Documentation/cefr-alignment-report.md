# CEFR Alignment Report: Writing 1, 2, 3 Cursusdoelen
**Date:** 2026-03-09 | **Purpose:** Stress-test cursusdoelen against CEFR descriptors and assessment tasks

---

## How to Read This Report

For each course, this report maps every cursusdoel against three things:
1. The **CEFR descriptor(s)** it draws from (with exact level and scale name)
2. The **assessment task** that's supposed to validate it
3. Whether these three elements (cursusdoel, CEFR descriptor, assessment task) actually form a coherent triangle

A mismatch in any corner of that triangle is a flag. An opportunity is a place where the CEFR offers language or concepts we could leverage but currently don't.

## Writing 1 — B1/B2 — Timed Test Only (90 min)

### Cursusdoel 1
> Je kunt heldere, samenhangende teksten schrijven over vertrouwde en niet-vertrouwde onderwerpen, passend bij het communicatieve doel, de doelgroep en genreconventies.

**CEFR source:** This draws from two scales:

- **Overall Written Production B1:** "Can produce straightforward connected texts on a range of familiar subjects within their field of interest, by linking a series of shorter discrete elements into a linear sequence."
- **Overall Written Production B2:** "Can produce clear, detailed texts on a variety of subjects related to their field of interest, synthesising and evaluating information and arguments from a number of sources."
- **Reports and Essays B1:** "Can produce short, simple essays on topics of interest."

**Assessment task:** Cambridge B2 First format. Part 1 = compulsory essay; Part 2 = choice (article, email/letter, report, review).

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** The timed test directly validates this. Students produce texts under controlled conditions in response to prompts that specify purpose, audience, and genre. The Cambridge B2 format is explicitly designed to test exactly this capability. The cursusdoel language ("heldere, samenhangende teksten") echoes B1 ("straightforward connected texts") while "vertrouwde en niet-vertrouwde onderwerpen" stretches toward B2 ("a variety of subjects"). This accurately reflects a B1/B2 range target.

**One note:** The cursusdoel mentions "genreconventies," which is appropriate since the Cambridge Part 2 tasks require genre awareness (a review reads differently from a report). However, the B1 descriptors don't mention genre conventions at all; that concept enters at B2 in the Creative Writing scale ("following established conventions of the genre concerned"). This means a student performing at pure B1 level might not demonstrate genre awareness. That's fine for assessment: it's what separates "at level" from "above level" in the rubric.

### Cursusdoel 2
> Je kunt schrijfstrategieën inzetten om teksten effectief te organiseren en structureren.

**CEFR source:** This draws from Production Strategies, specifically:

- **Planning B1:** "Can work out how to communicate the main point(s) he/she wants to get across, exploiting any resources available and limiting the message to what he/she can recall or find the means to express."
- **Planning B2:** "Can plan what is to be said and the means to say it, considering the effect on the recipient(s)." Also: "Can rehearse and try out new combinations and expressions, inviting feedback."

**Assessment task:** Timed test. Strategies are visible in the product (paragraph structure, cohesion devices, text organization) but NOT directly articulated by the student.

**Alignment verdict: ADEQUATE, with a known limitation.** The verb "inzetten" (apply/deploy) is well-chosen for W1 because the timed test can only show strategy *use*, not strategy *awareness*. You can see whether a student uses topic sentences, logical paragraph ordering, and cohesion markers. You cannot see whether they did so consciously. This is the right choice for a timed-only format; the more demanding "bewust inzetten en verantwoorden" is reserved for W2/W3 where P+Q&A validates conscious application.

**Opportunity:** The CEFR B2 Planning descriptor mentions "considering the effect on the recipient(s)," which is about audience awareness during planning. The current cursusdoel focuses on organizing and structuring. If you ever want to make the planning/audience link more explicit in the rubric (not the cursusdoel), the CEFR gives you language for it.

### Cursusdoel 3
> Je beheerst het geschreven Engels op ERK-niveau B1/B2.

**CEFR source:** This is a holistic reference to multiple qualitative scales:

- **Vocabulary Range B1+:** "Has a sufficient vocabulary to express him/herself with some circumlocutions on most topics pertinent to his/her everyday life."
- **Vocabulary Range B2:** "Can vary formulation to avoid frequent repetition, but lexical gaps can still cause hesitation and circumlocution."
- **Grammatical Accuracy B1+:** "Communicates with reasonable accuracy in familiar contexts; generally good control though with noticeable mother tongue influence."
- **General Linguistic Range B1+:** "Has a sufficient range of language to describe unpredictable situations, explain the main points in an idea or problem with reasonable precision."

**Assessment task:** Timed test. Language level is directly visible in the product.

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** A timed test is the most direct way to validate language level because it removes external support. The cursusdoel is deliberately holistic ("beheerst het geschreven Engels") rather than specifying grammar, vocabulary, or coherence separately. This gives rubric-level flexibility. The old W1 cursusdoelen listed separate CEFR descriptors for vocabulary, grammar, fluency, and coherence; the new version correctly abstracts this to the rubric layer.

## Writing 2 — B2/C1 — P+Q&A Only (20 min), Take-Home Creative Mimicry

### Cursusdoel 1
> Je kunt goed gestructureerde teksten schrijven met toepassing van genreconventies, en toon, stijl en register aanpassen passend bij doelgroep en doel.

**CEFR source:** This draws heavily from:

- **Overall Written Production C1:** "Can employ the structure and conventions of a variety of genres, varying the tone, style and register according to addressee, text type and theme."
- **Creative Writing B2:** "Can give clear, detailed descriptions of real or imaginary events and experiences marking the relationship between ideas in clear connected text, and following established conventions of the genre concerned."
- **Creative Writing C1:** "Can produce clear, detailed, well-structured and developed descriptions and imaginative texts in an assured, personal, natural style appropriate to the reader in mind."

**Assessment task:** Take-home creative mimicry (write an additional chapter in an author's style) + P+Q&A where student explains genre/style choices.

**Alignment verdict: STRONG, with nuance.** The creative mimicry task is an excellent test of this cursusdoel. To write convincingly in someone else's style, students must understand genre conventions, tone, and register deeply enough to reproduce them. The P+Q&A then validates that this understanding is conscious rather than accidental.

The nuance: the CEFR C1 descriptor says "a variety of genres" (plural) and "varying" (implying flexibility across contexts). The W2 task tests depth in *one* genre/style rather than breadth across many. This is a defensible pedagogical choice: proving you can precisely control style in one context demonstrates the underlying capability. The cursusdoel's use of "aanpassen" (adapt) rather than "variëren" (vary) now accurately reflects this. You're testing targeted adaptation, not broad variation.

The Creative Writing C1 descriptor explicitly mentions "imaginative texts in an assured, personal, natural style appropriate to the reader in mind," which maps almost perfectly to what the mimicry task requires. The student must write in a style appropriate to a specific readership (the book's audience).

**Opportunity:** The B2 Creative Writing descriptor mentions "marking the relationship between ideas in clear connected text," which is about textual cohesion. The mimicry task naturally tests this (can the student maintain the author's logical flow?), but it's not made explicit in the cursusdoel or rubric. If cohesion is something you want to assess in the P+Q&A (e.g., "How did you maintain the author's way of connecting ideas?"), the CEFR supports it.

### Cursusdoel 2
> Je kunt schrijfstrategieën bewust inzetten en verantwoorden.

**CEFR source:**

- **Planning B2:** "Can plan what is to be said and the means to say it, considering the effect on the recipient(s)."
- **Planning C1:** "Can, when preparing a more formal text, consciously adopt the conventions linked to the particular type of text concerned (e.g. structure, level of formality)."
- **Monitoring and Repair B2:** "Can correct slips and errors that they become conscious of, or that have led to misunderstandings. Can make a note of their recurring mistakes and consciously monitor for them."

**Assessment task:** P+Q&A. Students present their creative piece and justify their strategic choices orally.

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** The P+Q&A format is the ideal validation mechanism for "bewust inzetten en verantwoorden." The C1 Planning descriptor literally says "consciously adopt the conventions," and the P+Q&A is where you verify that consciousness. The old W2 used a written argumentative essay for this; the oral format actually adds an additional CEFR dimension (Sustained Monologue) while testing the same strategic awareness.

The word "verantwoorden" (justify/account for) goes beyond the CEFR descriptors themselves, which describe *what learners can do* but don't typically require *justification*. This is a deliberate and valid pedagogical addition: as future English teachers, these students need to articulate *why* they make language choices, not just make them. This aligns with Kennisbasis 2.2.2 ("deployment of productive strategies").

**Opportunity:** The Monitoring and Repair scale is currently untapped in the cursusdoelen. At B2, students "can make a note of their recurring mistakes and consciously monitor for them." This maps directly to what could happen in the P+Q&A: a student could discuss how they identified weaknesses in their draft and addressed them. If you want to give the P+Q&A more structure, "monitoring and repair" is a CEFR-grounded angle for discussing revision processes. This could be particularly powerful since the take-home format allows for revision, and the P+Q&A can explore what that revision process looked like.

### Cursusdoel 3
> Je kunt grammaticaal bewustzijn en geschreven Engels op ERK-niveau B2/C1 aantonen, en grammaticale structuren herkennen en verklaren zoals toegepast in eigen schrijfwerk.

**CEFR source:**

- **Grammatical Accuracy B2:** "Shows a relatively high degree of grammatical control. Does not make errors which cause misunderstanding, and can correct most of his/her mistakes."
- **Grammatical Accuracy C1:** "Consistently maintains a high degree of grammatical accuracy; errors are rare, difficult to spot and generally corrected when they occur."

**Assessment task:** Take-home product with 3 highlighted grammar elements (simple/medium/complex) + P+Q&A where student explains these choices.

**Alignment verdict: ADEQUATE, but this is the most novel cursusdoel.** The CEFR doesn't have a "grammatical awareness" or "metalinguistic knowledge" scale. The CEFR describes what learners *can do with* grammar (produce accurate language), not whether they can *explain* grammar. This cursusdoel goes beyond the CEFR by adding a metalinguistic dimension: students must identify, label, and explain grammatical structures.

This is entirely defensible for a teacher training program. The Kennisbasis 2.2.2 explicitly requires "consistent and accurate grammatical control," and future teachers need metalinguistic awareness to teach grammar to their own students. The CEFR simply doesn't cover this because it was designed for general language learners, not language teachers. The cursusdoel essentially says: "Show B2/C1 level grammar in your writing AND show you understand the grammar you're using."

The highlight-and-explain mechanism (replacing the old footnote system) is a smart way to operationalize this. In the old W2, students annotated grammar throughout; now they select 3 targeted elements from a categorized list. This is more focused and more assessable in a 15-minute P+Q&A.

**Flag:** The "simple/medium/complex" grammar selection list doesn't yet exist and depends on the grammar reader revision. Until that list is finalized, the exact scope of "grammaticale structuren herkennen en verklaren" is undefined. The cursusdoel itself is fine, but the assessment operationalization has a dependency.

## Writing 3 — C1/C2 — Timed Test (90 min) + Take-Home + P+Q&A (20 min)

### Cursusdoel 1
> Je kunt heldere, complexe teksten schrijven in een effectieve stijl, waarbij communicatief doel, doelgroep en genreconventies strategisch worden ingezet.

**CEFR source:**

- **Overall Written Production C2:** "Can produce clear, smoothly flowing, complex texts in an appropriate and effective style and a logical structure which helps the reader identify significant points."
- **Overall Written Production C1:** "Can produce clear, well-structured texts of complex subjects, underlining the relevant salient issues, expanding and supporting points of view at some length with subsidiary points, reasons and relevant examples."
- **Creative Writing C1:** "Can produce clear, detailed, well-structured and developed descriptions and imaginative texts in an assured, personal, natural style appropriate to the reader in mind."

**Assessment tasks:** Timed test (Cambridge C1 Advanced format) validates written production under pressure. Take-home (student choice: proposal or creative piece) + P+Q&A validates authorship, process, and strategic thinking.

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** This cursusdoel is the most CEFR-aligned of all nine. The phrase "heldere, complexe teksten" echoes C1/C2 directly. "Effectieve stijl" comes from C2. "Strategisch ingezet" reflects the progression from W1 ("inzetten") through W2 ("bewust inzetten") to W3 ("strategisch inzetten"), which mirrors CEFR's implicit progression from doing → doing consciously → doing strategically.

The dual assessment format (timed + P+Q&A) means both the product and the process are validated. The timed test proves the student can produce complex texts independently; the P+Q&A proves they understand why their choices work.

**Opportunity:** The C2 descriptor mentions "a logical structure which helps the reader identify significant points." This reader-guidance aspect could be an explicit rubric element for the timed test, especially for the essay/proposal genres. It distinguishes C1 writing (well-structured) from C2 writing (structured *for the reader's benefit*).

---

### Cursusdoel 2
> Je kunt schrijfstrategieën bewust inzetten en verantwoorden bij complexe schrijftaken.

**CEFR source:**

- **Planning C1:** "Can, when preparing a more formal text, consciously adopt the conventions linked to the particular type of text concerned."
- **Planning C2:** No descriptors available; see C1.
- **Monitoring and Repair C1:** "Can backtrack when they encounter a difficulty and reformulate what they want to say without fully interrupting the flow of language. Can self-correct with a high degree of effectiveness."

**Assessment task:** P+Q&A. Students justify strategic choices in the context of a complex writing task (proposal or creative piece).

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** The progression "inzetten → bewust inzetten en verantwoorden → bewust inzetten en verantwoorden bij complexe schrijftaken" across W1-W2-W3 is clean and mirrors CEFR's progression from B1 (communicating main points) through C1 (conscious convention adoption). The addition of "bij complexe schrijftaken" is important because it raises the bar: the strategies aren't just conscious (W2) but appropriate to complexity (W3).

Note that the CEFR Planning scale tops out at C1 with no separate C2 descriptors. This means at C2 level, the CEFR assumes planning is fully internalized and doesn't need separate description. For W3, this is actually what the rubric's "above level" should capture: strategies so integrated they appear effortless.

---

### Cursusdoel 3
> Je beheerst het geschreven Engels op ERK-niveau C1/C2 met consistente grammaticale beheersing van complexe taalvormen.

**CEFR source:**

- **Grammatical Accuracy C1:** "Consistently maintains a high degree of grammatical accuracy; errors are rare, difficult to spot and generally corrected when they occur."
- **Grammatical Accuracy C2:** "Maintains consistent grammatical control of complex language, even while attention is otherwise engaged (e.g. in forward planning, in monitoring others' reactions)."
- **General Linguistic Range C2:** "Can exploit a comprehensive and reliable mastery of a very wide range of language to formulate thoughts precisely, give emphasis, differentiate and eliminate ambiguity."

**Assessment task:** Timed test (direct evidence of language level under pressure) + take-home product (evidence of polished language) + P+Q&A (demonstrates control extends to oral register).

**Alignment verdict: STRONG.** The phrase "consistente grammaticale beheersing van complexe taalvormen" mirrors C2 Grammatical Accuracy almost verbatim. This cursusdoel also serves as the concentric anchor for W2's grammar awareness: if a student passes W3 at level, they're demonstrating C1 grammatical control, which implicitly covers W2's B2/C1 requirement.

The timed test is particularly powerful here because the C2 descriptor specifies maintaining control "even while attention is otherwise engaged." A 90-minute timed writing exam is exactly the kind of situation where attention is divided between content generation and language accuracy.

## Cross-Course Observations

### Progression is Clean
The three courses show a clear CEFR-grounded progression across all three cursusdoel lines:

**Line 1 (Written production):**
- W1: "produce clear, connected texts" → B1/B2 language
- W2: "author well-structured texts, adapting tone/style/register" → B2/C1 language
- W3: "author clear, complex texts in an effective style, strategically" → C1/C2 language

**Line 2 (Strategy):**
- W1: "apply writing strategies" → strategy visible in product
- W2: "consciously apply and justify" → strategy articulated orally
- W3: "consciously apply and justify for complex tasks" → same verb as W2, escalation via task complexity and dual assessment

**Line 3 (Language/Grammar):**
- W1: "written English at B1/B2" → holistic
- W2: "grammatical awareness at B2/C1 + identify and explain structures" → metalinguistic
- W3: "written English at C1/C2 with consistent grammatical control" → holistic again, but at higher level

### The W2 Grammar Cursusdoel is the Outlier (and That's Fine)
W2's Cursusdoel 3 is the only one that goes beyond what the CEFR describes. The CEFR measures language *proficiency*; W2 measures language *awareness*. This is a justified departure because the program trains teachers, not just language users. The CEFR was never designed for teacher education; the Kennisbasis fills that gap.

### CEFR Scales Not Currently Referenced
Several CEFR Production scales exist that the cursusdoelen don't draw from. Some of these are deliberate omissions; others might be opportunities:

**Compensating (B1-C2):** Strategies for maintaining communication when vocabulary or structures fail. Not relevant for written production assessment (students can revise), but could be discussed in P+Q&A ("What did you do when you couldn't find the right word?"). Low priority.

**Monitoring and Repair (B2-C2):** Self-correction and revision awareness. This is implicitly tested in the take-home products (students can revise) and could be an explicit P+Q&A angle. At B2: "Can make a note of their recurring mistakes and consciously monitor for them." At C1: "Can self-correct with a high degree of effectiveness." **Medium opportunity** for W2 and W3 P+Q&A prompts about revision process.

**Reports and Essays (B1-C2):** Currently only used for W1 (Cambridge format). W3's proposal option could explicitly reference the C1/C2 descriptors from this scale, which mention "presenting a case" and "critical appreciation of proposals." **Medium opportunity** for W3 rubric specificity.

### What the Cursusdoelen Don't Commit To (by Design)
The cursusdoelen deliberately avoid specifying:
- Specific genres (creative mimicry, proposal, essay) → stays in the assignment descriptions
- Specific grammar elements or lists → stays in the course materials
- Cambridge exam format details → stays in the test specifications
- Number of words or tasks → stays in the assessment criteria
- Specific books or reading lists → stays in the course materials

This is the right level of abstraction for ACO entry. These are curriculum-level goals, not assignment specifications.
