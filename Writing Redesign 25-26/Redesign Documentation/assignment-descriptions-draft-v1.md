## WRITING 1 — Timed Test

**Writing 1: Timed Test**
**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Aids:** None | **Result scale:** above level / at level / not at level

**Format**
You write two texts in 90 minutes. The test follows the Cambridge B2 First writing format:

- **Part 1 (compulsory):** You write an essay in response to a prompt. You are given a statement and two ideas related to it. You discuss both ideas, add one of your own, and explain which you think is most important. (140–190 words)
- **Part 2 (choice):** You choose one task from several options. Each task asks you to write a specific text type — such as an article, email/letter, report, or review — for a given audience and purpose. Topics may be familiar or unfamiliar. (140–190 words)

**What you are assessed on**

| Criterion | What this means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Communicative effectiveness** *(outcome 1)* | Your text achieves its communicative purpose. You write appropriately for the target audience and apply the conventions of the required genre (e.g., register in a letter vs. an article). |
| **Writing strategy application** *(outcome 2)* | You apply strategies to organise and structure your text effectively — for example, logical sequencing, cohesion between sentences, and clear paragraph unity. |
| **Language level** *(outcome 3)* | Your written English is at B1/B2 level. Your grammar and vocabulary are adequate for the task, and errors do not impede communication. |

**How to prepare**
Practice writing under timed conditions. Focus on planning before you write, choosing the right structure and tone for each text type, and managing your time across both parts. Familiarity with Cambridge B2 First writing tasks is recommended — practice materials are provided during the course.

**AI policy:** AI tools are not available during the test (controlled digital environment).

## WRITING 2 — Creative Mimicry + P+Q&A

**Writing 2: Creative Mimicry & Presentation**
**Result scale:** above level / at level / not at level

**The assignment**
You select a chapter from a published literary work and write a **continuation or missing scene** (1000–1500 words) that mimics the original author's voice. Your text should be well-structured and demonstrate that you can adapt your tone, style, and register to match the source text's audience and purpose.

**Grammar element**
You highlight **three grammatical structures** in your text — one simple, one medium, one complex (from the selection list provided during the course). During the P+Q&A, you identify and explain these structures as applied in your own writing.

**Submission**
- Upload your text and the completed **author declaration form** in Gradework by the deadline: **[deadline TBD — awaiting confirmation from Peter]**.
- If you do not submit on time, you cannot present in the scheduled P+Q&A slot and will need to present in the resit week.

**Presentation + Q&A (20 minutes)**
The P+Q&A is where you consciously justify the writing strategies you used. You present your work and answer questions covering:
- Your understanding of the source text and the stylistic techniques you identified
- How you adapted tone, style, and register to match the original
- Your writing process and the strategies you applied — and why
- Your explanation of the three highlighted grammatical structures

**What you are assessed on**

| Criterion | What this means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Genre awareness & application** *(outcome 1)* | You can author a well-structured text employing genre conventions, and adapt tone, style, and register to audience and purpose. |
| **Strategy articulation** *(outcome 2)* | You can consciously apply and justify writing strategies appropriate to your text — describing your methods, using terminology, and giving examples. |
| **Grammatical awareness & language level** *(outcome 3)* | You can identify and explain grammatical structures as applied in your own writing. Your written and spoken English demonstrates B2/C1 level. |

**AI policy:** You may use AI tools to polish and refine your text, but you must remain the author. Your ownership of the content and creative decisions is validated during the P+Q&A.

## WRITING 3 — Component 1: Timed Test (50%)

**Writing 3: Timed Test (50% of final result)**
**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Aids:** None | **Result scale:** above level / at level / not at level

**Format**
You write two complex texts in 90 minutes. The test follows the Cambridge C1 Advanced writing format:

- **Part 1 (compulsory):** You write an essay based on a prompt that includes a topic and input ideas to evaluate. You develop a clear argument considering multiple perspectives, in an effective style appropriate to the task. (220–260 words)
- **Part 2 (choice):** You choose one task from several options. Each task asks you to write a specific text type — such as a letter/email, proposal, report, or review — strategically addressing a given communicative purpose, audience, and context. Topics may be familiar or unfamiliar. (220–260 words)

**What you are assessed on**

| Criterion | What this means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Communicative effectiveness** *(outcome 1)* | You produce clear, complex texts in an effective style, strategically leveraging communicative purpose, audience, and genre conventions. |
| **Writing strategy application** *(outcome 2)* | You apply writing strategies appropriate to complex tasks — visible in how you structure arguments, guide the reader, and deploy rhetorical techniques. |
| **Language level** *(outcome 3)* | Your written English demonstrates C1/C2 level with consistent grammatical control of complex language. Your vocabulary is varied and your style is natural. |

**How to prepare**
Practice writing complex, well-argued texts under timed conditions. Focus on developing a clear position, writing strategically for a specific audience, and demonstrating range and control in your grammar and vocabulary. Familiarity with Cambridge C1 Advanced writing tasks is recommended — practice materials are provided during the course.

**AI policy:** AI tools are not available during the test (controlled digital environment).

## WRITING 3 — Component 2: Proposal + P+Q&A (50%)

**Writing 3: Proposal & Presentation (50% of final result)**
**Result scale:** above level / at level / not at level

**The assignment**
You write a **proposal** — a formal, argumentative text aimed at a professional audience. You identify a problem or opportunity, present a well-reasoned case, and propose a course of action. The proposal should demonstrate clear, complex writing in an effective style, with consistent grammatical control at C1/C2 level.

**Submission**
- Upload your proposal and the completed **author declaration form** in Gradework by the deadline: **[deadline TBD — awaiting confirmation from Peter]**.
- If you do not submit on time, you cannot present in the scheduled P+Q&A slot and will need to present in the resit week.

**Presentation + Q&A (20 minutes)**
The P+Q&A is where you consciously justify your writing strategies for this complex task. You present your proposal and answer questions covering:
- Your authorship — demonstrating that you wrote this text and made the key decisions
- The writing strategies you applied and why they were appropriate to this task
- Your rhetorical and structural decision-making (topic, argument, audience targeting)
- Your understanding and application of formal proposal conventions

**What you are assessed on**

| Criterion | What this means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Authorship & language level** *(outcomes 1 + 3)* | You can demonstrate that you authored a clear, complex text in an effective style. Your written and spoken English demonstrates C1/C2 level with consistent grammatical control of complex language. |
| **Strategy justification** *(outcome 2)* | You can consciously apply and justify writing strategies appropriate to this complex task — articulating your methods, evaluating their effectiveness, and discussing trade-offs. |
| **Decision-making** *(outcome 1)* | You can explain the rhetorical and structural choices in your proposal, showing how you strategically leveraged communicative purpose and audience. |
| **Genre application** *(outcome 1)* | Your text follows the conventions of a formal proposal and you can articulate what those conventions are and why they matter. |

**AI policy:** You may use AI tools to polish and refine your proposal, but you must remain the author. Your ownership of the content, argumentation, and structural decisions is validated during the P+Q&A.
