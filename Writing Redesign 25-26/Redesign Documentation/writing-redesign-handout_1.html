Writing Course Redesign

Team Consultation — January 2026
📚 Kennisbasis 2.2 — Productive Skills

Core Concept: The starting teacher produces various forms of language output in the target language at an average C2 level of the CEFR. This concerns:

2.2.1 Clear and coherent speaking and writing in the target language appropriate to the communicative purpose, target audience, and context

2.2.2 Deploying productive strategies, including:

  • Speaking and writing about familiar and unfamiliar topics
  • Deploying appropriate writing and speaking strategies to improve the quality of both informal and formal production
  • Deploying conventions appropriate to the genre concerned
  • Demonstrating consistent and accurate grammatical control, including in complex language forms. Errors are virtually absent and relate only to rarely used forms.

✍️ Writing 1 — Timed Test
Timed Test 60 minutes, supervised

Part A: Familiar Genre (15–20 min)

Professional communication in a known context. Students select one prompt from options such as:

  • Administrative email: Respond to a parent inquiry about a student's progress or classroom policy
  • Colleague memo: Inform team members about a schedule change or upcoming event
  • Professional request: Email to administration requesting resources or proposing a minor change

Genre is familiar from teacher training; tests ability to produce appropriate register and tone under time pressure.

Part B: Unfamiliar Topic with Sources (40 min)

Expository paragraph on a provided topic using 1–2 given sources.

  • Must include: 1 direct quote (grammatically integrated) + 1 paraphrase
  • APA in-text citations required
  • Topic requires engaging with source material (cannot answer from prior knowledge alone)
Rubric: Timed Test (3 criteria)
Criterion Validates What It Captures
Source Integration Outcome 2 Quotes grammatically integrated, paraphrases accurate, APA formatting correct
Communicative Effectiveness Outcome 1 (partial) Does the text achieve its purpose for the intended audience?
Language Level Outcome 4 Vocabulary range, grammatical accuracy, fluency at B1/B2
✍️ Writing 1 — Take-Home + Presentation + Q&A
Take-Home Assignment

Article for Semi-Formal Teacher Journal

A personal, argumentative piece written for a professional audience of fellow teachers. Students take a stance on a topic relevant to education and support it with evidence and reasoning.

  • Audience: Fellow teachers (not academic examiners)
  • Tone: Professional but personal; voice and perspective encouraged
  • Structure: More freedom than traditional expository essay; can use subheadings, anecdotes, rhetorical questions
  • Sources: Must integrate at least 2 sources using APA

⚡ Admissibility Check (Go/No-Go before Presentation + Q&A)

  • Formatting: Document properly formatted
  • Language level: Passes B2 "gut check" (no glaring errors)
  • APA reference list: Present for sources used
Presentation + Q&A 12.5 + 12.5 + 5 buffer = 30 min

Student presents their take-home article, explaining their choices. Followed by examiner questions probing authorship, process, and decision-making.

Presentation Elements (12.5 min)

Students address these elements (structured conversation, not rigid order):

  1. Communicative Purpose: What is the article trying to do? Who is the audience? How does the text achieve its purpose?
  2. Writing Process: Overall approach, preparation, tools used
  3. Genre Conventions — Content: Source selection (why these, why not others), quote vs. paraphrase choices with examples
  4. Genre Conventions — Structure: Topic sentence choices, argument selection, organizational decisions
  5. Detailed Writing Strategies: Specific strategies used (using terminology), concrete examples from text, how strategies serve genre conventions
Rubric: Presentation + Q&A (2 criteria)
Criterion Validates What It Captures
Genre Awareness Outcome 1 Can articulate communicative purpose, target audience (teachers), and genre conventions (content + structure)
Strategy Application Outcome 3 Can explain high-level writing process and name specific strategies with examples from their text

Authorship is not a separate criterion — if students can articulate genre awareness and strategies, they've demonstrated authorship. The format itself validates it.
