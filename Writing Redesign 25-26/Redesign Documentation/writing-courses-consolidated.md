# Writing Courses — Consolidated Design Document
**Status:** DRAFT v5 | **Date:** 2026-03-06 | **Purpose:** Single source of truth for ACO entry

---

## Foundation

### Kennisbasis 2.2: Productive Skills
The starting teacher produces diverse forms of language utterances in the target language at an average C2 level of the CEFR.

- **2.2.1** Clear and coherent writing, appropriate to communicative purpose, target audience, and context
- **2.2.2** Deployment of productive strategies; writing on familiar and unfamiliar topics; applying genre conventions; consistent and accurate grammatical control

### Assessment Philosophy: Dual-Lane Strategy
- **Lane 1 (Secured):** Timed test (W1, W3) and/or Presentation & Q&A (W2, W3) — validates language level, authorship, and strategy awareness under controlled conditions
- **Lane 2 (Unsecured):** Take-home writing product (W2, W3) — vehicle for demonstrating strategy application; assessed indirectly through P+Q&A defense

### Cross-Course Design Principles
- APA referencing is no longer explicitly tested in any course
- CEFR level outcomes reference the target range (e.g., B1/B2) with rubric levels mapped accordingly
- Writing 3 is concentric (passing W3 grants automatic exemption for both W1 and W2). This works because W3 operates at C1/C2 and includes both a timed test and a take-home + P+Q&A, covering all skills validated in the lower courses.
- Writing 2 is NOT concentric (passing W2 does not grant exemption for W1). W2 has no timed test, so it cannot validate the controlled writing skills that W1 assesses. Only W3 exempts W1.
- Grammar is implicit via CEFR in W1 and W3; explicit as part of a combined criterion in W2 (students demonstrate grammatical awareness as future teachers)
- Timed tests follow Cambridge exam conventions at the appropriate level

### Admissibility: Quality Declaration Form (W2, W3)
For courses with a take-home + P+Q&A component, students submit their work in Gradework along with an author declaration form. This is a checklist where students declare:
- They are the author of the work
- The APA reference list is complete (where applicable)
- The work has been properly edited for grammar and spelling
- Basic formatting and submission requirements are met

**Deadline:** Work + declaration must be uploaded **1 week before** the P+Q&A slot. This gives time to plan and schedule presentation slots.

**If not uploaded on time:** The student cannot present in the scheduled slot. They can present during test week or resit week outside the main block.

### AI Policy (All Courses)

**Assessment (Toetsing):**
The assessment format is the AI policy:
- **Timed tests (W1, W3):** Niet toegestaan — controlled environment, no devices
- **Take-home products (W2, W3):** Toegestaan met randvoorwaarden — AI may shape the text, but the student remains author; authorship and process are validated through the P+Q&A defense

**Learning process (Leerproces):**
- **W1 (timed test only):** Toegestaan met randvoorwaarden — AI use during the learning process is limited to getting feedback on writing the student has produced independently (e.g., identifying weak spots, getting revision pointers). Using AI to improve, reformulate, or generate text is not useful preparation for a timed test and is therefore not encouraged. Spell-check and brainstorming tools remain available but offer limited value for this course's assessment format.
- **W2 (take-home + P+Q&A):** Toegestaan met randvoorwaarden — AI can play a meaningful role in polishing and refining take-home texts, provided the student remains the author and maintains close engagement with the text. Students are expected to submit work that is properly edited and polished; AI can support this. However, outsourcing the cognitive work (idea generation, argumentation, structural decisions) to AI undermines the learning process and will be exposed during the P+Q&A.
- **W3 (timed test + take-home + P+Q&A):** Same split as above — for timed-test preparation, AI use is limited to feedback on own writing; for the take-home component, AI is allowed with the same authorship expectations as W2.

| 2 | Can consciously apply and justify writing strategies appropriate to the text | De student kan schrijfstrategieën bewust inzetten en verantwoorden |

### Assessment Components

**No timed test.** Language level validated in W1 (propedeutic) and W3 (CPE readiness).

**Component 1: Take-Home Creative Mimicry — Lane 2 (input for P+Q&A)**
- **Genre:** Stylistic imitation — students select a chapter from a literary work and write a continuation or missing scene (1000-1500 words) mimicking the original author's voice, pacing, and vocabulary
- **Grammar element:** Students highlight 3 grammatical structures in their text (one simple, one medium, one complex — from a provided selection list) that they will explain during the P+Q&A
- **Not a separate toets** — bundled with the P+Q&A
- **Quality declaration form** required upon submission in Gradework (1 week before P+Q&A)

**Component 2: Presentation + Q&A — Lane 1**
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Toetsvorm** | Assessment |
| **Weging** | 100 |
| **Toetsduur** | 20 minuten |
| **Resultaatschaal** | Boven niveau, op niveau, niet op niveau |
| **Opnemen in rooster** | JA |
| **Duration breakdown** | 15 min (presentation + Q&A) + 5 min buffer |
| **Test week logistics** | 3-hour blocks: 8 students x 20 min + 20 min break halfway |

**P+Q&A focus areas:**
- Source text understanding and technique identification
- Adaptation decisions (style, voice, pacing)
- Writing process and strategy justification
- Grammar element explanation (highlighted structures)

**Rubric levels:**
| Criterion | Maps to Outcome | At Level (B2+) | Above Level (C1+) |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Genre Awareness & Application** | Outcome 1 | Explains conventions; demonstrates awareness of audience; applies genre features | Nuanced understanding; evaluates effectiveness of genre choices |
| **Strategy Articulation** | Outcome 2 | Articulates methods used; uses terminology; gives examples | Evaluates strategy effectiveness; discusses trade-offs |
| **Grammatical Awareness & Language Level** | Outcome 3 | Identifies and explains B2/C1 structures in own work; language at B2+ | Sophisticated grammatical insight; language at C1+ |

**AI:** Allowed for take-home product; authorship validated through P+Q&A

## Key Changes from Previous Versions

| Change | What happened | Why |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **APA removed** | No longer an explicit learning outcome or assessment criterion | Meeting decision |
| **W1 simplified** | Timed test only; Lane 2 completely removed | Meeting decision |
| **W1 timed test** | Cambridge B2 First approach, 90 min | Avoids reinventing the wheel; fits learning outcomes |
| **W3 timed test** | Cambridge C1 Advanced approach, 90 min | Consistent with W1 approach at higher level |
| **W2 grammar reframed** | Footnotes replaced by highlight-and-explain (3 elements: simple/medium/complex); grammar + language level combined into 1 criterion | Students engage with grammar explicitly; simplifies rubric |
| **W2 outcomes reduced** | From 4 to 3 (grammar awareness + language level merged) | Combined criterion |
| **CEFR rubric design** | W1: B1+ at level, B2+ above. W2: B2+ at level, C1+ above. W3: C1 at level, C2+ above | Range matters |
| **Quality declaration form** | Student self-declaration checklist replaces teacher-driven admissibility check | Puts responsibility on student; ensures P+Q&A is productive |
| **"Author" verb dropped for W1** | No P+Q&A means no authorship validation format | Timed test inherently proves authorship |
| **AI policy = assessment format** | Timed tests: AI not available. Take-home: AI allowed. P+Q&A validates authorship. | Dual-lane design makes AI policy self-evident |
| **P+Q&A reduced to 20 min** | Was 30 min, now 15 + 5 buffer | Streamlined |

## Future Opportunities (from CEFR Alignment Report, March 2026)

These are CEFR-grounded ideas noted for future rubric/assignment development. They do NOT require changes to cursusdoelen.

- **W2/W3 P+Q&A — Monitoring and Repair:** CEFR B2 "Can make a note of recurring mistakes and consciously monitor for them"; C1 "Can self-correct with a high degree of effectiveness." Could inform P+Q&A questions about revision process (e.g., "What did you change between drafts and why?")
- **W3 Rubric — Reader-guidance at C2:** CEFR C2 Overall Written Production mentions "a logical structure which helps the reader identify significant points." Could be an explicit rubric differentiator between C1 (well-structured) and C2 (structured for the reader's benefit)
- **W1 Rubric — Audience awareness in planning:** CEFR B2 Planning mentions "considering the effect on the recipient(s)." Could strengthen the audience awareness rubric criterion
- **W2 P+Q&A — Textual cohesion in mimicry:** CEFR B2 Creative Writing mentions "marking the relationship between ideas in clear connected text." Could prompt P+Q&A question about how the student maintained the author's way of connecting ideas
- **Design decision (documented):** The strategy verb progression is inzetten (W1) → bewust inzetten en verantwoorden (W2) → bewust inzetten en verantwoorden bij complexe schrijftaken (W3). W2 and W3 share the same verb ("bewust") because the CEFR Planning scale tops out at C1 ("consciously adopt") with no separate C2 descriptors. The escalation from W2 to W3 comes from task complexity and dual assessment format, not from a higher tier of strategic consciousness
