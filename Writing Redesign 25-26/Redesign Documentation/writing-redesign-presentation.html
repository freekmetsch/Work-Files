Trigger for Change: Kennisbasis 2025/2026

2.2 Productieve vaardigheden (productie)

Kernconcept: De startbekwame leraar produceert uiteenlopende vormen van taaluitingen in de doeltaal op een gemiddeld C2 niveau van het ERK.

Core concept: The novice teacher produces various forms of language output in the target language at an average C2 level of the CEFR.

2.2.1

NL: Het helder en samenhangend spreken en schrijven in de doeltaal passend bij het communicatieve doel, de doelgroep en de context

EN: Clear and coherent speaking and writing in the target language appropriate to the communicative purpose, target audience, and context

2.2.2

NL: Het spreken en schrijven over vertrouwde en niet-vertrouwde onderwerpen • Het inzetten van passende schrijfstrategieën • Het inzetten van conventies passend bij een betreffend genre

EN: Speaking and writing about familiar and unfamiliar topics • Using appropriate writing strategies • Using conventions appropriate to a given genre

W1 Timed Test (60 min) — Detailed

Part A: Familiar (15-20 min)

No sources needed

Example prompts:

  • "Write formal email to Team Leader about scheduling conflict"
  • "Reply to parent asking about failed test"
  • "Memo to colleague about missed meeting"

Tests: Professional writing, audience awareness, formal register

Part B: Unfamiliar (40 min)

With sources provided

Example:

"Write summary paragraph on 'Screen Time and Deep Reading' for parent newsletter"

  • Given 1-2 sources (~300 words)
  • Must include: 1 quote, 1 paraphrase
  • APA in-text citations required

Tests: Reading comprehension, integration, neutral tone, citation mechanics

Rubric (3 Criteria)

Criterion At Level
Source Integration Quotes grammatically integrated • Paraphrases accurate • APA mostly correct
Communicative Effectiveness Achieves purpose • Appropriate for audience • Clear structure
Language Level (B1/B2) Vocabulary & grammar at level • Errors don't impede understanding

W1 Take-Home: Article for Informal Teacher Journal

Why this genre?
Clear audience (teachers) • Allows opinion about teaching • More accessible than abstract expository essay distinction

GO/NO-GO Check (Asynchronous: 3-5 days before P+Q&A)

Time investment: ~3-5 min per student

Criterion Pass Requirements
Formatting Basic document formatting (title, paragraphs, spacing)
Language Level Text contains no/minimal errors in spelling & basic grammar (B2 threshold)
APA Reference List Included, complete, and properly formatted
NO-GO? → Student doesn't use assessment opportunity (resubmission required)

W1 Presentation + Q&A (30 min)

Presentation (12.5 min)

Student addresses:

  1. Communicative purpose (what, why, who)
  2. Writing process (high-level approach, tools)
  3. Genre conventions — Content (source selection/rejection)
  4. Genre conventions — Structure (topic sentences, organization)
  5. Detailed strategies (with terminology & examples)

Q&A (12.5 min)

Teacher interrogates:

  • Claims made in presentation
  • Elements not yet covered
  • Alternatives considered
  • Decision rationales

Examples: "You selected [source X]. What made it suitable?" • "Walk me through this paragraph's connection to your purpose"

Rubric (2 Criteria)

Criterion At Level
Genre Awareness Can explain communicative purpose • Demonstrates audience awareness • Explains genre conventions applied
Strategy Application Articulates methods used (high-level + detailed) • Uses terminology appropriately • Gives examples from text

Grading: Both criteria "At Level" + GO/NO-GO pass → Pass • One "Not Yet" → Resubmission • Both "Not Yet" OR NO-GO fail → Resubmission

Part-Time Logistics Solution

Challenge: PT students have 2 hrs/week vs FT 4 hrs/week, but same assessment requirements
↓

Cross-Grading Solution

  • FT teachers assess PT students
  • PT teachers assess FT students
  • Distributes P+Q&A load evenly
  • Enables parallel sessions (4 teachers = 4 simultaneous P+Q&As)

Grading Load Comparison

Current System

  • 30-45 min marking essay
  • 15-30+ min follow-up (variable)
  • Unlimited extra meetings
  • Total: 45-75+ min (unpredictable)

Proposed System

  • 15-20 min timed test grading
  • 3-5 min GO/NO-GO check
  • 30 min P+Q&A (scheduled)
  • 5 min admin
  • Total: 50-55 min (fixed, predictable)

Mental load reduced: Verbal feedback in scheduled slots vs async written feedback spiral
