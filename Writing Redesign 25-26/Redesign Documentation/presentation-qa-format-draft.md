# Presentation + Q&A Format: Writing Courses Redesign

**Status:** DRAFT v0.2 | **Created:** 2025-01-29 | **Updated:** 2025-01-29 (voice notes integrated) | **For:** Team Leader Presentation (Jan 30)

---

## Overview

### Purpose
The Presentation + Q&A (P+Q&A) is a **Lane 1 (secured) assessment** that validates authorship and strategy awareness. Students present their take-home writing product, focusing on **process and decision-making**, not language quality. The Q&A probes deeper into the same elements.

### What P+Q&A Assesses
- **Authorship:** Student can explain what they wrote and why
- **Strategy awareness:** Student can verbalize writing strategies using appropriate terminology
- **Decision-making:** Student can articulate choices made and alternatives discarded
- **Genre awareness:** Student understands conventions and made conscious choices

**Q&A Format:** The Q&A is an interrogation of claims made in the presentation and follows up on elements not yet covered. Teachers use the rubric criteria to guide questioning, probing deeper where needed.

### What P+Q&A Does NOT Assess
- Language level (validated in timed test for W1/W3)
- Grammar accuracy
- Final product quality (that's Lane 2; we're assessing the thinking behind it)

## Timed Test Component (Writing 1 & Writing 3 Only)

### Purpose
The timed test is a **Lane 1 (secured) assessment** that validates:
- Language level (CEFR B1/B2 for W1, C1/C2 for W3)
- Source integration skills (APA formatting, quoting, paraphrasing)
- Communicative effectiveness (does the text work for its purpose?)

### Writing 1 Timed Test (60 minutes)

**Structure:**
- **Part A: Familiar Prompt** (15-20 min) - Formal/professional writing, no sources needed
- **Part B: Unfamiliar Prompt** (40 min) - Expository/summary with source integration

**Part A Examples (Choose One):**

1. **The Admin:** "You are a first-year teacher. You have noticed a scheduling conflict between your mentor hour and a mandatory workshop. Write a formal email to your Team Leader proposing a solution."

2. **The Parent:** "A parent has emailed you asking why their child failed a recent test. Write a formal, diplomatic reply explaining the grading criteria used."

3. **The Colleague:** "You missed a department meeting due to illness. Write a memo to your colleague asking for a summary of the key decision points regarding the upcoming project week."

**Part B Structure:**
- **Topic Example:** "The Impact of Screen Time on Deep Reading"
- **Input:** 1-2 short source texts (~300 words total)
- **Task:** Write a summary paragraph (150-200 words) for a school newsletter
- **Audience:** Parents
- **Must Include:** 1 direct quote, 1 paraphrase, correct APA in-text citation
- **Assesses:** Reading comprehension, integration skills, neutral tone, citation mechanics

### Writing 1 Timed Test Rubric (3 Criteria)

| Criterion | Not Yet at Level | At Level | Above Level |
|-----------|------------------|----------|-------------|
| **Source Integration** | Quotes not grammatically integrated; paraphrases inaccurate; APA errors undermine credibility | Quotes grammatically integrated; paraphrases accurate; APA mostly correct | Seamless integration; sophisticated paraphrase; APA technically correct |
| **Communicative Effectiveness** | Text unclear or doesn't achieve purpose; audience not considered | Text achieves purpose; appropriate for audience; clear structure | Highly effective; nuanced audience awareness; compelling |
| **Language Level (B1/B2)** | Vocabulary/grammar below B1; errors interfere with meaning | Vocabulary/grammar at B1/B2; errors don't impede understanding | Vocabulary/grammar at B2+; minimal errors |

### Writing 3 Timed Test (90 minutes)

**Structure:**
- Three paragraphs (vs. two in W1)
- 3 sources provided (vs. 1-2 in W1)
- Higher complexity topics
- Same format otherwise

### Writing 3 Timed Test Rubric (3 Criteria)

| Criterion | Not Yet at Level | At Level | Above Level |
|-----------|------------------|----------|-------------|
| **Source Integration** | Quotes not grammatically integrated; paraphrases inaccurate; APA errors undermine credibility | Quotes grammatically integrated; paraphrases accurate; APA technically correct | Seamless, sophisticated integration; synthesis of multiple sources |
| **Communicative Effectiveness** | Text unclear or doesn't achieve purpose; audience not considered | Text achieves purpose; appropriate for audience; clear structure | Highly effective; nuanced audience awareness; compelling |
| **Language Level (C1/C2)** | Vocabulary/grammar below C1; errors interfere with meaning | Vocabulary/grammar at C1/C2; errors don't impede understanding | Vocabulary/grammar at C2; virtually error-free |

**Note:** Writing 2 has no timed test component. Language level is validated in W1 (propedeutic) and W3 (CPE readiness).

## Genre-Specific Formats

### ðŸ“„ PROPOSAL (Writing 3)

**Presentation Elements (12.5 min)**

Students should address these elements (not necessarily in this order):

1. **Thesis/Main Claim**
   - "What is the central argument or proposal?"
   - "What problem are you addressing?"

2. **Hook/Engagement Strategy**
   - "How did you choose to draw readers in?"
   - "Why this approach to opening?"

3. **Argumentation Structure**
   - "What are your three main supporting arguments (the WHYs)?"
   - "How did you decide on this structure?"

4. **Source Integration**
   - "Which sources did you select and why?"
   - "Which sources did you discard and why?"
   - "Show one example of a quote and one paraphrase; explain the choice"

5. **Theoretical Framework**
   - "How did you frame key concepts for your reader?"
   - "What did you assume your reader already knows vs. needs explained?"
   - Example: "If your proposal involves 'fear of failure,' how did you introduce that concept?"

6. **Writing Strategy**
   - "What was your approach/process for writing this?"
   - "What strategies did you use?" (using provided terminology)

7. **Diction/Word Choice**
   - "Show one deliberate word choice and explain why"

**Q&A Prompts (12.5 min)**

Follow-ups on presentation elements:
- "You mentioned [X] as your hook. What other approaches did you consider?"
- "Why did you place [argument Y] second rather than first?"
- "You discarded [source Z]. What made it unsuitable?"
- "If you had more time, what would you change?"
- "What was the hardest decision you made in this piece?"
- "How does [specific paragraph] connect to your thesis?"

### âœï¸ CREATIVE PIECE: Murderless Mystery or Personal Essay (Writing 3)

**Presentation Elements (12.5 min)**

1. **Central Theme/Effect**
   - "What did you want the reader to experience or understand?"
   - "What's the emotional or intellectual core of this piece?"

2. **Opening Strategy**
   - "How and why did you choose to start here?"
   - "What does this opening establish?"

3. **Structure Decisions**
   - "Why does the story/essay begin and end where it does?"
   - "How did you decide on the structure (chronological, fragmented, etc.)?"

4. **Character/Voice Choices** (Mystery) or **Persona/Perspective** (Personal Essay)
   - "Which characters did you include and why?"
   - "Which characters/details did you cut ('kill your darlings') and why?"
   - "What narrative voice did you choose and why?"

5. **Literary/Writing Techniques**
   - "Identify 2-3 specific techniques you used" (using provided terminology)
   - "Show examples from your text"

6. **Genre Awareness**
   - "How does this piece fit the conventions of [mystery/personal essay]?"
   - "Where did you deliberately follow or break conventions?"

7. **Revision Decisions**
   - "What changed between your first draft and final version?"
   - "What did you cut or add, and why?"

**Q&A Prompts (12.5 min)**

- "You mentioned using [technique X]. Why was that the right choice for this moment?"
- "The ending does [Y]. What effect were you going for?"
- "You cut [character/scene]. What made you decide it wasn't working?"
- "How did you decide on this level of detail in [section]?"
- "What would you do differently if you wrote this again?"
- "Where in the piece are you most satisfied with your writing? Why?"

### ðŸŽ¨ CREATIVE MIMICRY (Writing 2)

**Note:** Writing 2's P+Q&A is more comprehensive and focused on justification of specific application of creative, stylistic, and genre conventions because it replaces the previous contrastive essay assignment. Without a timed test to validate language level, this presentation carries more weight in demonstrating student competency.

**Presentation Elements (12.5 min)**

1. **Source Text Understanding**
   - "What are the defining features of [original author]'s style?"
   - "Which elements did you identify as essential to capture?"

2. **Technique Application**
   - "Which specific techniques from the original did you replicate?"
   - "Show examples from your text alongside the original"

3. **Adaptation Decisions**
   - "How did you adapt the style to your own content/story?"
   - "Where did you struggle to maintain the style?"

4. **Structure/Plot Choices**
   - "Why does your story start and end where it does?"
   - "How does your structure mirror or diverge from the original?"

5. **Character/Setting Choices**
   - "Which characters did you include? Why?"
   - "What 'darlings' did you kill?"

6. **Writing Process**
   - "What was your approach to writing this piece?"
   - "How did you balance imitation with originality?"

**Q&A Prompts (12.5 min)**

- "The original uses [technique X]. Show me where you did this and explain your choice"
- "How is your piece recognizably 'in the style of' [author] to a reader?"
- "Where did you find it hardest to maintain the style? Why?"
- "You chose [this plot element]. How does that connect to the original's themes?"
- "What would [original author] think of your piece? Why?"
- "If you had to explain this piece to someone who hasn't read [original], what would you say?"

### ðŸ“ ARTICLE FOR INFORMAL TEACHER JOURNAL (Writing 1)

**GO/NO-GO ADMISSIBILITY CHECK (Done asynchronously: few days before)**

Before the presentation can proceed, the student's submitted article must pass these criteria:
- **Formatting:** Basic document formatting (title, paragraphs, spacing)
- **Language Level:** Text contains no/minimal errors in terms of spelling/basic grammar (B2)
- **APA reference list:** Included, complete, and properly formatted

**If GO/NO-GO fails:** Student cannot proceed with P+Q&A; must revise and resubmit.

**Presentation Elements (12.5 min)**

Students address these elements (structured conversation, not rigid order):

1. **Communicative Purpose**
   - "What is this article about? What is it trying to do?"
   - "Who is the audience?" (other teachers reading the journal)
   - "How does the text achieve its purpose for this audience?"

2. **Writing Process (High-Level)**
   - "What was your overall approach to writing this piece?"
   - "How did you prepare? What tools did you use?"

3. **Genre Conventions: Content**
   - "Which sources did you select and why?"
   - "Which sources did you discard and why?"
   - "Show one example of a quote and one paraphrase; explain why you chose to quote vs. paraphrase"

4. **Genre Conventions: Structure**
   - "Why this topic sentence?" (point to specific paragraph)
   - "Why did you decide on these specific arguments/points?"
   - "How did you organize the article? Why this structure?"

5. **Detailed Writing Strategies**
   - "What specific strategies did you use?" (using terminology bank)
   - "Give concrete examples from your text"
   - "How did these strategies help you meet the genre conventions?"

**Q&A Prompts (12.5 min)**

The Q&A interrogates claims made in the presentation and follows up on elements not yet covered:

- "You mentioned selecting [source X]. What made it suitable for this audience?"
- "Walk me through this paragraph: how does it connect to your main purpose?"
- "You used [strategy Y]. What alternatives did you consider?"
- "If you were writing for an academic journal instead, what would change?"
- "What was the hardest decision you made in writing this? Why?"
- "Show me one place where you revised significantly. What changed and why?"

**Note:** Language quality is NOT assessed in P+Q&A (validated in timed test). Focus is on authorship demonstration.

## Strategy Terminology Bank

Students need shared vocabulary to discuss writing strategies. Provide this list in advance:

### Process Strategies
- Brainstorming / mind-mapping
- Outlining / planning
- Freewriting / discovery drafting
- Reverse outlining (after drafting)
- Iterative revision
- Peer feedback integration

### Structural Strategies
- Topic sentences / paragraph unity
- Transitions / cohesion devices
- Thesis-driven organization
- Chronological / thematic / problem-solution structure
- Hook / lead types (anecdote, question, statistic, quote)

### Source Integration Strategies
- Quote sandwich (introduce â†’ quote â†’ analyze)
- Paraphrase for flow
- Strategic source selection
- Synthesis (combining multiple sources)

### Creative Writing Techniques
- Show don't tell
- Sensory detail
- Dialogue
- Pacing / tension
- Character development
- Point of view choices
- Imagery / figurative language

### Revision Strategies
- "Kill your darlings" (cutting beloved but unnecessary content)
- Reading aloud
- Reverse outline check
- Peer review incorporation

## Rubric Framework

### Presentation + Q&A Rubric (Writing 1, 2, 3)

**Structure:** 2 criteria assessed during P+Q&A + GO/NO-GO check (done asynchronously)

| Criterion | Not Yet at Level | At Level | Above Level |
|-----------|------------------|----------|-------------|
| **Genre Awareness** | Cannot explain communicative purpose, audience, or genre conventions; responses vague | Explains communicative purpose clearly; demonstrates awareness of audience (teachers); explains genre conventions applied | Demonstrates nuanced understanding of how text achieves purpose for this specific audience; evaluates genre choice effectiveness |
| **Strategy Application** | Cannot identify or explain strategies used; no appropriate terminology | Articulates methods used (high-level process + detailed strategies); uses terminology appropriately; gives examples from text | Demonstrates deeper awareness by evaluating strategy effectiveness; explains integration of tool use with own authorship; discusses trade-offs |

**GO/NO-GO Check (Pass/Fail):** Formatting ✓, Language level (B2: no/minimal spelling/grammar errors) ✓, APA reference list (included, complete, properly formatted) ✓

**Grading Logic:**
- Both criteria "At Level" or above + GO/NO-GO pass → Pass
- One criterion "Not Yet at Level" → Not yet passed; requires resubmission
- Both criteria "Not Yet at Level" OR GO/NO-GO fail → Not yet passed; requires resubmission

**Note:** Authorship is not a separate criterion. If a student can articulate genre awareness and strategies, they've demonstrated authorship. The P+Q&A format itself makes authorship visible.

## Logistics Notes

### Cross-Grading Solution
- FT teachers assess PT students; PT teachers assess FT students
- Distributes load and solves DT scheduling constraints
- Enables parallel P+Q&A sessions (4 students simultaneously with 4 teachers)

### Scheduling
- Build P+Q&A slots into final weeks of course
- Use test week / resit week slots as needed
- FT: Can sacrifice some session hours (4 hrs/week available)
- PT: Aim to minimize session sacrifice; leverage cross-grading

### Recording (Optional/TBD)
- Consider recording P+Q&A for moderation purposes
- Discuss privacy implications
- Could be useful for borderline cases

## Open Questions for Team Leader Input

1. **Rubric detail level:** Is 4 criteria sufficient, or do we need more granularity?

3. **Recording policy:** Record all, record borderline cases only, or no recording?
4. **Presentation aids:** Can students bring notes? Use slides? Or fully from memory?
5. **Rescheduling policy:** What if a student can't attend their slot?
